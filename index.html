Frequently Asked Questions

"World Wide Women's Wisdom" is a concept that highlights the collective knowledge, strength, and experiences of women from diverse cultures and backgrounds worldwide. It celebrates their contributions in various fields, including leadership, education, arts, and social change.

Women's wisdom is vital because it brings unique perspectives, nurtures communities, and fosters innovation. It encompasses traditional knowledge, life experiences, and professional expertise that shape societies for the better.

You can explore books, TED Talks, podcasts, and global organizations that focus on women's empowerment, leadership, and history. Some great sources include UN Women, Lean In, and Women’s History Month resources.

Women share wisdom through storytelling, mentorship, books, speeches, online communities, activism, and cultural traditions.

Women of Vision

1098 - 1179
Hildegard of Bingen Hildegard of Bingen (1098-1179 also called Hildegard von Bingen) was a nun, an author, a composer, and a mystic. Today she is probably best remembered for her music, which is still performed.
1343 - 1416
Julian of Norwich Julian of Norwich ( c. 8 November 1342 – c. 1416) is considered one of England 's greatest Christian mystical writers . She is venerated by the Church of England , the Lutheran Church , and the Catholic Church.
1347 - 1380
Catherine of Siena A mystic and theologian who corresponded with popes, influenced political and religious reform, and wrote The Dialogue, a profound spiritual work.
